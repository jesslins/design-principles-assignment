Negotiation

The negotiations for Treaty 1 started on the 27th of July, 1871. The First Nations were told, "Your Great Mother, therefore, will lay aside for you 'lots' of land to be used by you and your children forever. She will not allow the white men to intrude upon these lots. She will make rules to keep them for you, so that as long as the sun shall shine, there shall be no Indian who has not a place that he can call his home."

Throughout the negotiations for this treaty, there was a constant difficulty to form a mutual understanding of concepts that were unknown to the First Nations, such as "reserves" and "surrender". Treaty 1 was officially signed on the 3rd of August, 1871.

Historic Terms

  • Gratuity (one-time payment) of $3
  • Yearly annuity of $15 per family of five
  • Promise of schools on reserves
  • Prohibited introduction and sale of liquor on reserves
  • Nations required to "cede, release, surrender, and yield up to her Majesty the Queen"
